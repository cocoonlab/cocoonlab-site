How Cocoon fits into your workflow.

From first conversation to export‑ready concepts in three clear steps.

  1. 1

    Capture your site & constraints

    Start from an address, parcel ID, or uploaded plans. Cocoon ingests zoning overlays, meeting notes, and your own standards to build a living knowledge base for the project.

    • Automatic summaries from meetings and email threads
    • Site geometry, orientation, and access conditions
    • Target program, budget bands, and risk flags
  2. 2

    Generate & explore options

    Cocoon produces multiple zoning‑aware schemes and keeps them organized by goals: yield, daylight, phasing, or risk. Steer with prompts or constraints; Cocoon keeps track of the reasoning.

    • Multiple floor‑plan and massing families per site
    • Filters for unit mix, efficiency, and compliance status
    • Design notes and assumptions stored alongside each option
  3. 3

    Validate, align & export

    Before you commit, Cocoon compiles zoning pre‑checks, feasibility bands, and a clean narrative of decisions. Export to CAD/BIM and share crisp concept packs with clients, cities, and investors.

    • Visual zoning summary and code citations
    • Quick density, cost, and schedule snapshots
    • Exports to DWG, IFC, RVT, PDF, and CSV

Frequently asked questions.

Everything architects and developers typically ask us before their first pilot.

What kinds of projects is Cocoon best for?

Cocoon is optimized for early‑stage design and feasibility on residential, mixed‑use, hospitality, industrial, and office projects. It shines whenever there are multiple stakeholders, tight constraints, and pressure to align quickly.

Which regions and zoning codes are supported?

We’re rolling out localized zoning and building code libraries city by city. At launch we support a focused set of municipalities, then expand based on partner demand. For new regions, Cocoon can operate using your own rule sets and references.

How does Cocoon integrate with our existing tools?

Today, Cocoon exports design geometry, schedules, and reports into common formats like DWG, IFC, RVT, PDF, and CSV. Dedicated connectors for specific CAD/BIM and finance tools are on our roadmap.

How is our project data stored and used?

Your workspaces are private to your organization. We don’t use your client data to train public models, and we follow industry best practices for encryption in transit and at rest. Enterprise customers can request stricter controls and retention policies.

Does Cocoon replace architects or planners?

No. Cocoon is a copilot. It captures messy context, generates options, and makes constraints visible so licensed professionals can exercise judgment with more time and better information.

Can Cocoon capture meetings and project communications?

Yes. Cocoon can ingest meeting notes and other project materials to keep a living project record. Upcoming modules add structured transcription and task extraction so nothing gets lost between conversations.

What does onboarding look like?

Smaller teams can start self‑serve in minutes. Growth and Enterprise plans include guided onboarding: we calibrate templates to your markets, run pilot projects with your team, and help define internal best practices.

Can we run Cocoon on highly confidential projects?

Yes. We support stricter access controls, customer‑managed keys, and private deployments where required. We’ll work with your security team to match their expectations.
